Who is Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle?

Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle is a glamorous American attorney, TV personality, legal analyst, and author. She married Californian politician Gavin Newsom and was First Lady of San Francisco during Newsom’s first two years as mayor.

She is an anchor and the co-host for ‘The Five’ and ‘Outnumbered’ on the Fox News Channel. She was also a part of the most-watched cable news program of 2015 ‘The O’Reilly Factor’.

Kimberly has been part of other criminal stories shows as a guest host.

In July 2018, Guilfoyle abruptly left Fox News. The network released a brief, single-sentence statement: “Fox News has parted ways with Kimberly Guilfoyle.”

Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle: Early Life, Childhood

Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle was born on March 9, 1969, in San Francisco, to a Puerto Rican mother, Mercedes Guilfoyle,  and an Irish father, Anthony “Tony” Guilfoyle. She has a mixed ethnicity of Irish and Puerto Rican.

Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle spent her childhood in  San Francisco and was raised in her parents’ Roman Catholic faith. She grew up in the Mission District of San Francisco and in Westlake, Daly City, California, USA.

At which university did Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle attend?

Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle did her schooling at Mercy High School. Guilfoyle graduated magna cum laude from the University of California, Davis, and in 1994, received her Juris Doctor (J.D.) from the University of San Francisco School of Law.

She later studied at Trinity College, Dublin in Ireland. While there, she published research on international children’s rights and European Economic Community law.

While she was pursuing law as her academic career, she worked as an intern in the district attorney’s office in San Francisco. Kimberly Ann Guilfoyle was hired as a prosecutor for a brief period of time after she completed her legal studies.

During her early days, she also worked as a model for local department stores. She also modeled for Victoria’s Secret’s Lingerie for a bridal magazine.
